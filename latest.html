About

Exploring protein dynamics and flexibility.

I am a PhD graduate working at the interface of protein molecular modeling, structural bioinformatics and biophysical predictions. My research combines molecular dynamics (MD) simulations, NMR ensembles, AlphaFold2 models and large-scale normal mode analysis (NMA) to understand how gradations in protein flexibility shape function and interpretation.

This animation shows triplicate MD simulations of α-1 acid glycoprotein (AGP), an acute-phase immunomodulatory protein whose five N-glycans are absent in crystal structures due to their high flexibility. The simulations compare AGP (unglycosylated) and gly-AGP (fully glycosylated) across cancer-associated mutant (R101W). On the left, RMSF for a key fragment shows how backbone flexibility shifts across replicas: glycosylation can increase motion through protein–glycan interactions, while mutations may enhance or dampen flexibility depending on local context. Together, the replicas and RMSF view illustrate AGP’s characteristic context-dependent, ambiguous flexibility.

This figure (left) compares multiple measures of protein flexibility across large-scale datasets, linking computational predictions to experimental observations. We evaluated how AlphaFold2 pLDDT scores, NMR-derived RCI/S² metrics, MD-derived fluctuations, and Normal Mode Analysis (NMA)-computed RMSF relate to each other. NMA, applied to an equilibrium conformer, generates low-frequency collective motions through eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the Hessian matrix; RMSF was computed using the first 200 non-trivial modes. This enables a fast, consistent estimation of functional protein motions and their agreement with experimental dynamics. This plot (right) shows the Pearson correlation between NMA-computed RMSF and NMR S² across AlphaFold2 structures and their corresponding NMR ensembles. Correlations improve substantially when NMA is run on NMR ensemble models rather than on single AlphaFold2 structures. Ensembles provide multiple conformers that better represent solution-state uncertainty, allowing NMA to capture experimentally observed dynamics more faithfully.

Protein time scales

Inspired by pixel-accurate solar system maps, this strip places protein motions along a single time axis, from femtosecond (fs) bond vibrations to hours-long conformational and functional changes. Scroll horizontally to move along the axis.

Time scale → from ultrafast vibrations to slow functional motions
Scale ≈ 1.0 × 10⁻¹⁵ s (1.0 fs)
Bond vibrations
10⁻¹⁵–10⁻¹² s (fs–ps)
Fast stretching and bending of bonds and angles. These motions are typically averaged out in most experiments, but define the high-frequency limit of protein dynamics.
Side-chain flickers
10⁻¹²–10⁻⁹ s (ps–ns)
Local χ-angle rotations and backbone wobbles. They modulate local packing and show up in NMR order parameters and short MD trajectories.
Loop breathing
10⁻⁹–10⁻⁶ s (ns–μs)
Opening and closing of loops and turns, altering the exposure of binding pockets, glycosylation sites and epitopes.
Secondary-structure shifts
10⁻⁶–10⁻³ s (μs–ms)
Helix–coil transitions and β-structure rearrangements. These gradations are central to ambiguous or “fuzzy” protein regions.
Domain motions
10⁻³–10⁰ s (ms–s)
Hinge and shear motions between domains that often control allosteric regulation and long-range communication.
Conformational exchange
10⁰–10² s (s–min)
Exchange between long-lived conformers. Detected as line broadening and relaxation dispersion in NMR.
Binding / unbinding
10⁻³–10² s
Association and dissociation of ligands, glycans and partners. Kinetics here link conformational dynamics directly to affinity.
Turnover & remodeling
10²–10⁴ s (min–h)
Slow processes such as folding after synthesis, degradation, trafficking and large-scale remodeling within cells.

Projects

GitHub projects & tooling

Most of my code is hosted on GitHub under github.com/Bhawnadixit . Below are the main themes; the full list of repositories is on my profile.

  • Gradations of protein dynamics – analysis pipelines
    Scripts and notebooks for processing NMR ensembles, AlphaFold2 models, and normal mode analyses; computing correlations between experimental and predicted dynamics, and generating gradation profiles.
  • AGP molecular dynamics & glycosylation
    Workflows for building glycosylated and unglycosylated AGP systems, running multi-replica MD, and quantifying backbone and glycan flexibility.
  • Protein biophysics tooling & notebooks
    Utilities for integrating b2bTools predictions with structural models and exploring relationships between predicted features and ensemble behaviour.
